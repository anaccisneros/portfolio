Problem Statement

How might we design an intuitive carrier sourcing tool for integration within ITS’s in-house softwares?

Resources

Upon receiving the project, Devin provided me with several valuable resources, including the Project PRD, a Figma Mid-Fi Prototype, and an Expansive Azure Project Scope list. Most importantly, the weekly 1:1 meetings for progress reviews and direct access to Devin (who sat across from me) significantly enhanced our output speed.

Research

User research highlighted parallels between carrier sourcing and online shopping experiences. This insight led to the idea that a simplified, shopping-like flow would help users navigate carrier options effortlessly. Key influences in design choices included Hoka for clean UI, Outlook for user familiarity, Temu and Amazon for a streamlined user selection process.

Ideation

Filter Functionality:

Filter to mimic shopping sites appearing on the left. Results chosen to appear at the top of the screen with functionality to clear.

Condensed View Options:

For users needing a quick overview, expand all/collapse all options available.

Quick Copy Paste:

Meeting with LSE’s 8/20/24 revealed Copy Paste buttons by carrier phone, email, and MC/DOT numbers.

Email Blast:

Design to more closely mimic Outlook.

CSV Download Option:

Meeting with LSE’s on 9/19/24 revealed they CSV files on mail merger.

Carrier View Limit:

Meeting with LSE’s 8/20/24 revealed some carriers receive an email as spam if sent to more than 30 recipients. Aids in preventing an overwhelming amount of text on screen.
